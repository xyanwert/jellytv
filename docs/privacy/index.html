JellyTV Privacy Policy

Last updated: 5 July 2026

JellyTV is a native Apple TV client for Jellyfin media servers, together with an iPhone/iPad companion app that acts as a remote control. This policy explains, plainly, what the apps do and do not do with your data.

We do not collect your data

JellyTV and its companion app do not collect, store, transmit, or sell any personal information. There are no analytics, no advertising, no trackers, and no third-party SDKs that phone home. We have no servers of our own, and we never receive your information.

Where your data goes

The apps communicate only with the Jellyfin server you configure — a server you or someone you trust runs. Your server address, account credentials, and viewing activity are exchanged directly between the app on your device and that server. That connection is not visible to us. Please review the privacy practices of the Jellyfin server you connect to, as it is operated independently of JellyTV.

On-device storage

Settings and sign-in details are stored locally on your device (for example in the system keychain and app preferences) so you don't have to re-enter them. This information stays on your device and is removed if you delete the app.

Local network access

The companion app may ask for permission to access your local network. This is used solely to discover and control your Apple TV and Jellyfin server on the same network. No local network information leaves your device.

Children's privacy

Because the apps collect no data, they collect no data from children.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, the updated version will be published at this URL with a new "last updated" date.

Contact

Questions? Open an issue on the project's GitHub repository.

JellyTV is an unofficial, independent client and is not affiliated with or endorsed by the Jellyfin project. "Jellyfin" is used for descriptive purposes only.
